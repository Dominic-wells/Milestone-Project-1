Don't Go At IT Alone...

“Alone, we can do so little; together, we can do so much.” - Helen Keller

Sooner or later you're going to need help let's face it, you can't do everything by yourself, right?

During the apocalypse, you may choose to survive alone or you may try to form a group with like-minded survivors. If you decide on enlisting fellows into your cause (You really should have some friends at the end times), you will want to make sure they are loyal, competent, intelligent and brave. Sadly you will want to avoid anyone that can't (won't) contribute anything or decrease your chances of survival.

As a general rule, groups are always superior to lone individuals or pairs, regardless of the logistical and group dynamic problems that can come with mid-size to larger groups.

After the apocalypse, life will become drastically different from what it currently is (if you're not already living in a warzone that is). The differences may seem extensive, but people still have the same basic needs; they will need to eat, they will still get sick, and there still needs to be some form of order. Because of this, there are a large amount of day to day tasks most people will not be able to complete as a lone survivor.

To Group Or Not To Group That Is The Question

The advantages and disadvantages of groups

Pros Cons
More sets of hands to help out More mouths to feed
Better fighting team Chance of fights within the team
More intimidating to enemies Draws more unwanted attention
More skills available Chance of backstabbing
Increased defensive shifts More space to defend

Building an Effective Team

Teamwork makes the dream work... -John C. Maxwell

To have a great chance of survival it would make sense to have a great team! You need individuals who can function and live together on a long-term basis, without ending up at each other's throats.

For this reason, it's best to start out with people who are already part of your circle of buddies or family. You've probably already talked to these people, at least some, about the zombie apocalypse and survival. So you've got a good basis to work with.

At some point, you'll find that you need skills that don't exist in your circle of survivors. That leaves you with two options; someone in the team learns those skills, or you go looking outside of that circle to find people who have the skills you need.

Should you pick the second option, start by assessing them before attempting to build a friendship and bring them into the team. You need to know if they are hiding anything and if you can get along with them, not just if they have the skills you need.

On the subject of skills make sure to enlist or train survivors with the following skills;

Leadership
Strong all-around skills, can make the tough decisions.
Survival
A true expert in urban and wilderness environments.
Fighting
Weapon knowledge, can hold his/hers own in a fight and can train others.
Gardening Skills
Knows when, what and where to plant.
Medical
Can sew deep cut, cast a broken arm and diagnose an illness.
General Repairs
Can repair mechanics to carpentry and can fabricate new items.
Fishing
Able to use all types of rods, nets and traps.
General Skills
Lockpicking, reading and understanding maps and compasses.

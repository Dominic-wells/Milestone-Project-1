Don't Go At IT Alone...

“Alone, we can do so little; together, we can do so much.” - Helen Keller

Sooner or later you're going to need help let's face it, you can't do everything by yourself, right?

During the apocalypse, you may choose to survive alone or you may try to form a group with like-minded survivors. If you decide on enlisting fellows into your cause (You really should have some friends at the end times) you will want to make sure they are loyal competent, intelligent and brave. Sadly you will want to avoid anyone that can't (won't) contribute anything or decrease your chances of survival.

As a general rule, groups are always superior to lone individuals or pairs, in spite of the logistical and group dynamic problems that can come with mid-size to larger groups.

After the apocalypse, Life will become drastically different from what it currently is (if you're not living in a warzone that is). The differences may seem extensive, but people still have the same basic needs; they will need to eat, people will still get sick, and there still needs to be some form of order. Because of this, there are a large amount of day to day tasks most people will not be able to complete as a lone survivor.

To Group Or Not To Group That Is The Question

The advantages and disadvantages of groups

Pros Cons
More sets of hands to help out More mouths to feed
Better fighting team Chance of fights within the team
More intimidating to enemies Draws more unwanted attention
More skills available Chance of backstabbing
Increased defensive shifts More space to defend

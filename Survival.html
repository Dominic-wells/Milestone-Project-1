Fight, Run or Hide..

"To know your Enemy you must become your Enemy" -Sun Tzu-The Art of War

When you find yourself facing the end times it's good to know not only how to defend yourself from an attacking zombie but how to defend yourself from the big wild world. A group... A herd of zombies if you will, will pose a highly unique set of challenges to your life but So will transforming into an ice block after wearing the wrong protective clothing, in the wrong shelter at the wrong time of the year.

Okay… so it’s unlikely to happen, but knowing what it takes to survive isn't all that bad right?

Where would you go? What would have in your kit? Would you have the skills needed to rebuild?

Water

There's no solid set amount of water we need to drink each day as we all have different needs depending on the environment, our daily activity and overall health but you should aim to have at least 1.2 Liters per day.

Boiling water is a great method of purifying water, bring it to the boiling point and boil for 10 minutes, and let the water cool before drinking..Durr.

Water purification tablets are also a lifesaver follow the manufacturer's guidance

Food

Don't cook your cat!.. well not just yet, maybe start with raiding your freezer/fridge as the Frozen/cooler items have a much lower shelf life when not kept at the appropriate temperature.

Before you grab your bow and arrows go open your cupboards you're looking for about 2,000 calories a day for women and 2,500 for men.

Rice, Beans, canned goods, freeze-dried and MREs (Meal, Ready to Eat) are all foods you should have stored away ready as they have a long shelf life and are easy to ration. Growing your food might be an option if you have a guarded settlement set up.

Fire

Heat is paramount to your survival. You will quickly find that fire is an excellent way to warm yourself up when you are stuck out in the wilderness. Incorrectly handled, fire can be very damaging. You must respect fire."

Fire can disinfect your water potentially saving you from falling sick. Fire is an important way to make food items edible and to clean food items of parasites and bacteria if you are uncertain about how clean they are.

Using fire in the right way can provide warmth, light and comfort. You can also weaponise fire to protect yourself from predators and zombies!

How to Build a Fire

Pick Your Spot

Pick a good spot to build a fire. You're looking for a base clear and dryish to avoid starting a brush fire and to make it easier for the spark to catch.

Gather Your Kindling And Wood

Hunt out and collect twigs, bark, leaves and small branches making sure you find the driest as you possibly can.

Build Your Fire

Pile your Kindling (Small twigs and leaves) under your larger pieces of wood to make a nest-like shape. Keep the nest safe from wind and as dry as possible.

Ignite!

Light the Kindling nest with a flame source-matches, flint and steel, lighters and fire source you have at hand.

Blow

Once you catch a spark or flame you may need to feed the fire with a gentle amount of oxygen by carefully blowing into the kindling nest.

Weapons

Nukes, RPGs, Shotguns and Assault rifles? Not too easy to come by in England (Thank God) But what we lack in heavy weapons we make up in household items including but not limited to;

  • Hammers
  • Kitchen Knives
  • Rolling Pins
  • Baseball Bats
  • Crowbars
  • Gardening Tools

Having access to Archery sporting goods such as crossbows would put you at an advantage.

The real dark horse when it comes to Uk accessible zombie weapons is the common Slingshot. Surprisingly powerful, quiet and with an almost endless supply of ammo, what more could you ask for!

First-Aid

It's important to have a well-stocked and up to date first aid kit on your base and or a smaller kit on your person on missions.

Stay calm in the situation and seek basic medical training BEFORE the situation.

Save yourself first, never put yourself in danger, you can't save anyone when you're dead right?

Always treat the most life-threatening conditions first. Save Bobby with the cut off arm before stave with the cut finger first, you know what I'm sayin.

Medication

1.Prescriptions that are needed for specific conditions such as diabetes or blood pressure (insulin, enalapril)

2.Antibiotics to treat bacterial infections that are unlikely to clear up without antibiotics could take too long to clear without treatment and or carry a risk of more serious complications (Amoxicillin, Cefalexin)

3.Pain relief will be vital. If you are in too much pain you will be unable to face the gruelling challenges of a post-apocalyptic world (Codeine)

4.Not a fully inclusive list but it's worthwhile to have a large supply of over the counter medication such as Imodium for anti-Diarrhea and paracetamol for light pain relief.
